Bez tig


'Bez tig' a collaboration between Oscar Coyoli and Rodrigo Velasco, both grew up in Mexico City and are currently based in Montréal. Bez tig means tiger in Zapotec, an intelligent and agile feline that the autochthonous from Oaxaca blessed. This particular strength, balance and lightness of the animal is reflected in the energetic hybrid of nostalgic soundscapes, vocal explorations, afrolatin live coded polyrhythms (e.g. tumbao, dembow, montuno) and audio-reactive 3D visuals created through live coding enabling the celebration of an emergent light ritual.



Sound is improvised by playing with previously explored chord progressions, besides improvised singing, vocal explorations and improvised live coded percussions, both musicians use algorithmic (TidalCycles, SuperCollider) and analog processes. Regards visuals, Coyoli have been creating 3D objects that Rodrigo overlay with algorithmic shapes, glitching them through ese2 during the performance.
